Lighting & Illumination
Lighting defines the volume and emotional weight of your subject.

1. Volumetric
Volumetric lighting tells the AI to render light as a physical substance in the air, creating visible "god rays" or shafts of light. This requires atmospheric particles like dust, fog, or smoke to scatter the light, resulting in a highly cinematic, three-dimensional atmosphere.

2. Chiaroscuro
Borrowed from classical painting, this adjective forces extreme contrast between deep, inky shadows and bright, sharp highlights. It eliminates mid-tones, using shadows to sculpt the subject and create a dramatic, moody, and highly theatrical appearance.

3. Bioluminescent
This creates a self-emitting, organic glow from within a subject, usually in cool tones like cyan, green, or violet. It tells the AI to treat organic matter (like plants, creatures, or fungi) as the primary light source, creating a magical or deep-sea alien aesthetic.

4. Dappled
Dappled lighting introduces a broken, speckled light pattern, typically caused by sunlight filtering through a canopy of leaves. It creates a soft, organic texture over the scene, adding a sense of natural tranquility and a clear indication of a time of day and environment.

5. Ethereal
Ethereal lighting forces a soft, glowing, and almost over-exposed brightness that feels delicate and otherworldly. It usually diffuses harsh shadows, resulting in a heavenly, dreamlike, or ghostly aesthetic where light seems to wrap around subjects.

6. Rim-Lit
Rim lighting places a strong light source directly behind the subject, outlining their silhouette with a bright "halo" or rim of light. It is incredibly effective for separating a subject from a dark background and emphasizing their physical contours.

7. Neon-Bathed
This applies highly saturated, artificial light sources—usually pinks, blues, and purples—that cast long, colored shadows. It gives the image a distinct cyberpunk, synthetic, or modern nocturnal urban feel.

8. Sun-Drenched
This adjective bathes the scene in intense, warm, and highly saturated daylight. It creates sharp, hard shadows and high contrast, evoking the feeling of a hot summer afternoon or an arid desert environment.

9. Iridescent
Iridescence tells the AI to render surfaces so that their colors change depending on the angle of view or the light. It creates a shimmering, multi-colored reflection, similar to a soap bubble, an oil slick, or a peacock feather.

10. Luminescent
Unlike standard light sources, luminescence gives a soft, ambient glow to an object without the harshness of a bulb or the sun. It makes materials look like they are gently radiating light from within.

11. Soft-Focus
This mimics a specific photographic lens effect where the lighting and edges are intentionally slightly blurred and glowing. It reduces harshness and contrast, often used to create a romantic, vintage, or dreamy atmosphere.

12. High-Key
High-key lighting floods the scene with bright, even light, virtually eliminating shadows. It creates a clean, sterile, optimistic, or highly commercial aesthetic, often used in beauty photography or minimalist design.

13. Low-Key
The opposite of high-key, this adjective dominates the image with dark tones and shadows, using only minimal light to reveal specific details. It is essential for creating suspense, mystery, or a somber mood.

14. Ambient
Ambient lighting relies on the natural, existing light in a scene without any directional, artificial spotlights. It results in soft, even, and realistic lighting that feels grounded and observational.

15. Cinematic
While broad, "cinematic" triggers a combination of dramatic lighting, shallow depth of field, and a specific color grade. It tells the AI to light the scene as if it were a high-budget movie, often utilizing backlighting and contrasting color temperatures.

16. Golden-Hour
This specifies the warm, soft, and long-casting directional light that occurs just after sunrise or just before sunset. It washes the scene in gold, orange, and amber tones, creating a nostalgic and romantic feeling.

17. Blue-Hour
Blue-hour dictates the cold, deeply saturated blue and purple ambient light that occurs during twilight, just before full darkness. It creates a serene, melancholy, or moody urban atmosphere.

18. Halation
Halation mimics vintage film where bright light sources "bleed" past their boundaries into the darker areas of the image, often with a slight red or orange tint. It adds a nostalgic, analog warmth to digital generations.

19. Harsh
Harsh lighting creates abrupt, razor-sharp transitions between light and shadow. It mimics a bare flash bulb or the midday sun, highlighting every flaw and texture for a gritty, uncompromising look.

20. Caustic
Caustic lighting generates the dancing, intersecting webs of light created when light refracts through a curved, transparent surface, like water or glass. It is perfect for underwater scenes or macro shots of liquids.

📐 Composition & Perspective
These adjectives dictate how the AI arranges the elements within the frame and where it places the "camera."

21. Isometric
Isometric perspective renders a 3D scene without perspective distortion—parallel lines never converge. It creates a detached, "video game" or architectural diorama aesthetic where everything is viewed from a raised, angled vantage point.

22. Symmetrical
Symmetry forces the AI to mirror the left and right (or top and bottom) sides of the image perfectly. It creates a sense of unnatural order, imposing authority, or Wes Anderson-style quirky perfection.

23. Asymmetrical
Asymmetry deliberately unbalances the frame, placing visual weight off-center. This creates a more dynamic, natural, and kinetic composition that draws the viewer's eye across the image.

24. Macro
Macro dictates extreme close-up photography, blowing up tiny details to fill the entire frame. It is highly effective for showing the microscopic textures of insects, eyes, fabric, or water droplets, often with a very shallow depth of field.

25. Panoramic
This forces an ultra-wide aspect ratio and field of view, capturing vast, sweeping landscapes or sprawling cityscapes. It emphasizes scale and the environment over individual subjects.

26. Bird’s-Eye
A bird's-eye view places the camera directly above the subject, looking straight down. It flattens the world into a map-like 2D plane, emphasizing geometry, patterns on the ground, and a sense of detachment.

27. Worm’s-Eye
This places the camera at ground level, looking sharply upward. It makes subjects loom over the viewer, emphasizing their massive scale, dominance, or imposing nature.

28. Minimalist
Minimalism strips the composition down to its absolute bare essentials, utilizing massive amounts of empty "negative space." It focuses the viewer entirely on a single subject, color, or geometric form.

29. Cluttered
Cluttered or maximalist composition fills every inch of the frame with objects, details, and visual noise. It creates a sense of chaos, lived-in reality, or overwhelming abundance.

30. Foreshortened
Foreshortening creates an extreme, exaggerated perspective where objects closer to the camera appear massively larger than objects further away. It creates a dynamic, aggressive, and 3D "in-your-face" visual effect.

31. Telephoto
A telephoto perspective compresses the distance between the foreground and the background, making distant objects appear much closer and larger than they are. It flattens the image and is great for stacking visual elements.

32. Wide-Angle
Wide-angle distorts the edges of the frame and makes spaces feel vastly larger and deeper than they actually are. It is excellent for cramped interiors or epic environmental establishing shots.

33. Rule-of-Thirds
While a structural rule, using this adjective tells the AI to place the main subject off-center, aligning with imaginary grid lines. It ensures a classic, highly pleasing, and professional photographic composition.

34. Framed (Sub-framing)
This instructs the AI to use elements within the environment (like a window, an archway, or tree branches) to create a visual frame around the primary subject, adding depth and voyeurism.

35. Vertiginous
This creates a composition that induces a sense of vertigo or dizziness. It usually involves extreme heights, sweeping leading lines that vanish into an abyss, and a distorted sense of gravity.

36. Juxtaposed
Juxtaposition forces the AI to place two highly contrasting elements side-by-side (e.g., a pristine robot in a muddy swamp). It is a conceptual composition tool used to highlight differences.

37. Layered
A layered composition explicitly separates the image into distinct foreground, middle-ground, and background elements. This forces the AI to create a deep, three-dimensional scene rather than a flat image.

38. Centered
Unlike the rule of thirds, centering places the subject dead in the middle of the frame. It creates a confrontational, portrait-like, and highly focused image.

39. Cropped
Using "tightly cropped" forces the AI to cut off edges of the subject, intentionally leaving parts out of the frame. This creates intimacy, tension, and a feeling that the image is just a glimpse of a larger world.

40. Kinetic
A kinetic composition uses diagonal leading lines and implied motion to make the still image feel like it is moving rapidly. It is essential for action scenes or sports imagery.

🧱 Texture & Materiality
These adjectives define the "tactile" quality of the surfaces in your generated art.

41. Matte
Matte surfaces absorb light rather than reflecting it, resulting in a smooth, dull, and non-shiny finish. It gives objects a modern, sophisticated, or velvety appearance, eliminating specular highlights.

42. Glossy
Glossy materials are highly reflective, bouncing light back and creating sharp, bright highlights. This texture is used to simulate wetness, fresh paint, plastics, or polished surfaces.

43. Rough-Hewn
This adjective implies materials that have been crudely chopped, carved, or broken, usually stone or wood. It adds a primitive, rustic, and highly uneven physical texture to surfaces.

44. Weathered
Weathered implies that a material has been subjected to the elements over a long period. The AI will add textures of erosion, rust, fading, peeling paint, and general decay.

45. Pristine
Pristine forces the AI to render surfaces as completely flawless, untouched, and brand new. It removes all dust, scratches, and imperfections, ideal for futuristic or high-end commercial renders.

46. Translucent
Translucent materials allow some light to pass through them, but diffuse it so objects behind are not clearly visible. It is perfect for rendering frosted glass, wax, human skin, or thin fabrics.

47. Opaque
Opaque materials block all light from passing through. While it is the default for many objects, specifying it ensures solid, heavy, and impenetrable surfaces.

48. Granular
Granular textures consist of tiny, distinct particles. The AI will render surfaces that look like sand, salt, coarse dirt, or heavy film grain, adding a gritty, noisy tactile quality.

49. Viscous
Viscous defines a thick, sticky, and slow-moving liquid texture. It is ideal for rendering honey, thick mud, magma, or alien slime, complete with heavy droplets and thick reflections.

50. Crystalline
Crystalline forces geometric, sharp-edged, and highly refractive textures. It makes materials look like quartz, diamonds, or ice, scattering light into geometric prisms.

51. Metallic
Metallic surfaces reflect light similarly to a mirror but often with a colored tint (like gold or copper). It emphasizes the hardness of an object and captures reflections of the surrounding environment.

52. Woven
Woven textures tell the AI to render interlacing threads, yarns, or fibers. It is essential for creating realistic fabrics, wicker, or carbon fiber, giving the surface a distinct micro-pattern.

53. Porous
Porous materials are filled with tiny holes or air pockets. The AI will render surfaces that look like sponges, pumice stone, or bone, creating a highly complex and organic micro-texture.

54. Velvety
Velvety textures absorb light uniquely, often appearing darker where light hits directly and lighter on the edges. It creates a soft, plush, and luxurious feel, applicable to fabrics or even flower petals.

55. Corroded
Corrosion is a specific type of weathering that implies a chemical breakdown, like intense rust or acid burns. It adds flaky, orange/brown textures to metals and a sense of structural failure.

56. Gelatinous
Gelatinous materials are semi-solid, wobbly, and usually somewhat transparent. This adjective gives organic subjects a squishy, jelly-like, and slightly unnatural physical presence.

57. Fibrous
Fibrous materials are made of long, stringy threads. It is perfect for rendering raw muscle, torn wood, or coarse ropes, emphasizing structural lines within the material itself.

58. Fleshy
Fleshy forces a material to look like living tissue—soft, slightly translucent, and organic. When applied to non-organic things (like a "fleshy chair"), it creates deep body-horror or surrealism.

59. Tactile
Using the word tactile tells the AI to exaggerate all textures in the image, making the surfaces look so realistic and pronounced that the viewer feels they could reach out and touch them.

60. Impasto
Borrowed from painting, impasto dictates that the "paint" (or texture) is laid on the surface so thickly that brush or palette knife strokes are clearly visible, creating a physically raised, sculptural 3D texture.

🎨 Color Palette & Tone
Color adjectives dictate the overall hue, saturation, and color grading of the final output.

61. Monochromatic
A monochromatic palette restricts the entire image to various shades, tints, and tones of a single color. It creates a highly stylized, unified, and atmospheric look, forcing the viewer to focus on light and texture.

62. Polychromatic
Polychromatic forces the use of many different, often clashing, colors. It creates a vibrant, overwhelming, and highly energetic visual experience, similar to a kaleidoscope.

63. Muted
Muted colors are mixed with gray or their complementary colors to reduce their intensity. They create a sophisticated, quiet, melancholy, or vintage atmosphere, avoiding bright, distracting hues.

64. Saturated
Saturated colors are pure, intense, and vivid. This adjective tells the AI to crank up the intensity of every hue, resulting in a hyper-real, comic-book, or aggressively vibrant aesthetic.

65. Pastel
Pastel colors are highly desaturated but very light, mixed with a lot of white. They create a soft, gentle, spring-like, or "candy-colored" aesthetic, often associated with whimsy or childhood.

66. Sepia-Toned
Sepia replaces all colors with shades of reddish-brown. It instantly ages an image, mimicking 19th-century photography and evoking a deep sense of history and nostalgia.

67. Earthy
An earthy palette relies on colors found in nature—browns, olive greens, ochres, and warm grays. It grounds the image, giving it a natural, organic, and rugged feeling.

68. Jewel-Toned
Jewel tones are deep, rich, and highly saturated colors like emerald green, sapphire blue, and ruby red. They impart a sense of luxury, royalty, mystery, and opulence.

69. Fluorescent
Fluorescent colors are unnaturally bright and appear to emit their own light. They create a synthetic, raver, or toxic aesthetic, pushing colors beyond natural boundaries.

70. High-Contrast
High-contrast color palettes place extreme opposites next to each other (e.g., pure white next to pure black, or bright orange next to deep teal). It makes the image punchy, dynamic, and visually aggressive.

71. Low-Contrast
Low-contrast palettes use colors that are very close to each other in brightness and hue. The image appears washed out, soft, hazy, and peaceful, with no harsh visual transitions.

72. Ombré
Ombré dictates a smooth, seamless gradient transition from one color to another across the image or subject. It creates a modern, sleek, and highly designed aesthetic.

73. Achromatic
Achromatic strictly means "without color," resulting in pure black, white, and gray. Unlike "black and white," which can sometimes be tinted, achromatic ensures a completely neutral grayscale image.

74. Neon
Neon palettes utilize electric, glowing colors like hot pink, lime green, and cyan against dark backgrounds. It is the defining color scheme for cyberpunk, synthwave, and nightlife aesthetics.

75. Triadic
A triadic palette uses three colors that are evenly spaced around the color wheel (e.g., red, yellow, and blue). It creates a vibrant but highly balanced and harmonious color scheme, popular in pop art and comics.

76. Analogous
Analogous palettes use colors that are next to each other on the color wheel (e.g., blue, blue-green, and green). This creates a highly serene, comfortable, and unified visual experience.

77. Tinted
A tinted image has a slight wash of a specific color over the entire frame (e.g., "blue-tinted"). It unifies the image under a single emotional color grade, common in modern cinema.

78. Washed-Out
Washed-out colors look severely faded by the sun or overexposure to light. It gives the image an aged, bleak, or hazy summer memory vibe, removing the punch from darker tones.

79. Iridescent (Color)
When used specifically for color, this dictates a pearlescent palette where pinks, blues, and greens shift and blend softly together, like the surface of an opal.

80. Stark
A stark palette is severe and unforgiving, usually utilizing harsh blacks, blinding whites, and maybe one aggressive primary color (like blood red). It creates a clinical, intense, or graphic novel feel.

🎭 Mood, Atmosphere & Style
These adjectives provide the emotional context and historical art movements that guide the AI's "vibe."

81. Melancholic
Melancholy forces a mood of quiet, reflective sadness. The AI will generally respond by using cooler, muted tones, rain or fog, and subjects that appear isolated or deep in thought.

82. Whimsical
Whimsy introduces a playful, quirky, and slightly surreal storybook quality. It often results in curvy architecture, bright but soft colors, and an overall sense of childlike wonder and harmlessness.

83. Dystopian
Dystopian dictates a ruined, oppressive, or decaying future. It triggers industrial textures, smog, brutalist architecture, and a bleak, desaturated color palette to convey societal collapse.

84. Utopian
The opposite of dystopian, utopian triggers an idealized, perfect world. It results in clean, sweeping, futuristic architecture, lush greenery integrated into cities, and bright, optimistic lighting.

85. Surreal
Surrealism blends the aesthetics of reality with dream logic. It instructs the AI to combine unrelated objects, distort physics (like melting clocks), and present impossible scenarios with photorealistic fidelity.

86. Nostalgic
Nostalgia evokes a sentimental longing for the past. The AI achieves this by mimicking vintage film stocks, soft lighting, and retro props, creating a warm, hazy "memory" aesthetic.

87. Ominous
Ominous creates a feeling that something bad is about to happen. It utilizes heavy shadows, looming shapes, oppressive skies, and unsettling, asymmetrical compositions.

88. Tranquil
Tranquility forces absolute peace and calm. The AI will use horizontal lines, still water, soft lighting, and balanced, spacious compositions to lower the visual heart rate.

89. Frenetic
Frenetic energy is chaotic, fast-paced, and wildly unorganized. It triggers motion blur, cluttered compositions, sharp angles, and clashing colors to simulate panic or extreme speed.

90. Somber
Somber implies a heavy, serious, and gloomy atmosphere. It is darker and less romantic than melancholy, often using deep grays and blacks to convey grief or solemnity.

91. Eerie
Eerie is unsettling and spooky in a quiet way. It relies on the "uncanny valley," unnatural stillness, subtle fog, and slightly "off" lighting to make the viewer uncomfortable.

92. Jubilant
Jubilant forces a feeling of intense, explosive joy. It triggers bright, warm colors, dynamic upward-sweeping compositions, and an abundance of light to convey celebration.

93. Claustrophobic
Claustrophobia creates a sense of being trapped. The AI achieves this by tightly framing the subject, bringing walls or background elements aggressively close, and using heavy shadows to crush the space.

94. Majestic
Majesty implies awe-inspiring scale and grandeur. It utilizes sweeping wide angles, dramatic skies, and dominant subjects (like mountains or castles) viewed from slightly below.

95. Intimate
Intimacy forces a close, personal connection with the subject. It uses tight cropping, warm ambient light, and shallow depth of field to make the viewer feel like they are in the subject's personal space.

96. Photorealistic
Photorealism demands that the AI output looks exactly like a photograph captured by a high-end camera, prioritizing accurate physics, realistic skin textures, and flawless lighting over stylized art.

97. Painterly
Painterly tells the AI to ignore photographic reality and instead mimic the strokes, blending, and imperfections of traditional canvas painting, focusing on light and color over sharp details.

98. Cell-Shaded
Cell-shading mimics the look of 2D comic books or anime in a 3D space. It removes subtle gradients, using flat blocks of color separated by bold, dark outlines for a graphic, illustrative feel.

99. Low-Poly
Low-poly mimics early 3D video game graphics, constructing the entire image out of a small number of flat, geometric polygons. It creates a charming, retro-digital, and faceted aesthetic.

100. Glitchy
Glitch art intentionally introduces digital errors, such as pixel sorting, chromatic aberration, data-moshing, and scan lines. It creates a chaotic, tech-noir aesthetic that breaks the "fourth wall" of the digital medium.
