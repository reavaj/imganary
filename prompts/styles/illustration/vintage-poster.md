# Vintage Travel / Propaganda Poster

The bold graphic style of WPA-era travel posters, Soviet constructivism, and mid-century advertising. Built for impact at a distance.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Dramatic, simplified. Strong directional light creating bold shadow shapes. No subtle gradients — light is a graphic element.
- **Composition:** Strong vertical or diagonal thrust. Foreground subject large, background simplified to geometric shapes. Text areas integrated into the design.
- **Color palette:** Saturated, limited — 4 to 6 flat colors. Deep navy, burnt orange, cream, forest green. Colors separated like screen-printing layers.
- **Textures & materials:** Lithograph grain, slight ink bleed on edges. Flat color with visible overprint areas. Aged paper patina.
- **Mood:** Heroic, optimistic, commanding. Everything is larger than life.
- **Rendering:** Simplified shapes, stylized figures. No photorealism — everything reduced to its most iconic form. Strong silhouettes.

## FLUX keywords
vintage lithograph travel poster, flat bold color blocks with visible screen-print registration, limited 4-5 color palette each a solid unmodulated fill, strong geometric simplification of all forms, hand-lettered display typography integrated into composition, WPA Federal Art Project style or 1950s airline poster, paper aging yellowed edges foxing marks, halftone dot pattern in midtones, bold graphic silhouettes against flat color sky, propaganda art poster composition single heroic focal point, no photographic detail no gradients no soft blending
