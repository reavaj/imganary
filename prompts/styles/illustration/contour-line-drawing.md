# Contour Line Drawing

Contour line drawing emphasizes outlines and edges to define subjects, omitting internal details and shading. Originating as a fundamental drawing exercise, it distills forms to their essential shapes, prioritizing accuracy of line over realistic rendering. This technique focuses the viewer's attention on the subject's form and spatial relationships.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Light is implied only through the suggestion of form, not explicitly rendered with shadows or highlights.
- **Composition:** Simple, direct framing that highlights the subject's outline. Negative space is often as important as the subject itself.
- **Color palette:** Typically monochromatic (black on white), but can incorporate a single color for emphasis.
- **Textures & materials:** The texture is defined entirely by the character and weight of the line. Paper is a common background.
- **Mood:** Minimalist, focused, analytical; can range from clinical to expressive depending on the line quality.
- **Rendering:** Emphasizes outlines and edges, excluding internal details and shading. Line weight variation can be used to suggest depth or form.
