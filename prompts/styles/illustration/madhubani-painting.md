# Madhubani Painting

Madhubani painting, also known as Mithila art, is a traditional Indian folk art originating from the Mithila region. Practiced primarily by women, it is characterized by its vibrant colors, intricate patterns, and depiction of mythological figures, nature, and everyday life. The art form often uses natural dyes and pigments on surfaces like walls, cloth, and paper.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Flat, even lighting; no shading or highlights.
- **Composition:** Two-dimensional, with figures often filling the entire space. Geometric patterns and borders are common. Hierarchical scaling, where size indicates importance.
- **Color palette:** Bright, bold primary and secondary colors like red, yellow, blue, green, and black. Colors are often used symbolically. White is used for filling.
- **Textures & materials:** Flat, opaque colors on surfaces like handmade paper or cloth. Textures are created through intricate linework and patterns rather than shading. Natural pigments derived from plants and minerals.
- **Mood:** Celebratory, devotional, and narrative. Often depicts themes of fertility, prosperity, and social harmony.
- **Rendering:** Stylized and symbolic representation of figures and objects. Bold outlines, double lines, and decorative patterns. Lack of perspective and realism.
