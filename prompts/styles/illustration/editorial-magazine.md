# Editorial Magazine Illustration

The sophisticated illustration style found in The New Yorker, The Atlantic, or NYT opinion pages. Conceptual, witty, slightly surreal.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Flat or subtly directional. No photorealistic lighting — light serves the concept, not realism.
- **Composition:** Bold, graphic layouts with strong focal points. Negative space as a design element. Often incorporates visual metaphor.
- **Color palette:** Limited palette — 3 to 5 colors maximum. Often muted earth tones with one saturated accent. Risograph-inspired color overlaps.
- **Textures & materials:** Paper grain visible. Halftone dots, pencil texture under digital color. Screen-printed quality. Slightly rough edges.
- **Mood:** Intellectual, wry, thought-provoking. The image is an argument, not just a picture.
- **Rendering:** Flat color fields with minimal shading. Bold outlines or no outlines. Figures slightly abstracted — not photorealistic proportions.
