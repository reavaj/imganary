# Dystopian

The decayed, hostile, and desolate futures of Alien, Blade Runner, Mad Max, and the oppressive regimes of Star Wars. Worlds where humanity clings to survival amid industrial ruin, authoritarian control, and existential dread.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Harsh overhead fluorescents in sterile corridors, failing emergency lighting casting red and amber pools. Exterior scenes use diffused, overcast daylight — a sun choked by ash, smog, or perpetual cloud cover. Deep shadows with minimal fill. Practical light sources only — no "beautiful" light exists in these worlds.
- **Composition:** Oppressive scale — vast industrial structures dwarfing human figures. Long, converging corridors (Nostromo, Death Star). Empty, wide shots emphasizing isolation. Low angles looking up at monolithic architecture. Symmetry suggesting totalitarian order, or complete asymmetry suggesting collapse.
- **Color palette:** Desaturated earth tones — rust, concrete gray, olive drab, dried blood brown. Cold institutional blues and greens for interiors (Alien, THX 1138). Sickly yellow-green atmospherics (toxic skies, chemical haze). Occasional searing orange from fire or molten industry (Mustafar, Fury Road). No clean whites — everything is stained.
- **Textures & materials:** Corroded steel, dripping condensation, cracked concrete, peeling paint. Industrial grating, exposed ductwork, analog switches and CRT monitors. Organic decay — mold, rust bloom, biological residue. Dust and ash coating every surface. Brutalist architecture — raw, unfinished, imposing.
- **Mood:** Dread, abandonment, suffocation. The feeling of being watched or hunted. Quiet before violence. The weight of a world that has already ended or is grinding toward its end. Beauty only in the terrible sublime — a mushroom cloud, a planet-killing weapon, a hive of alien horror.
- **Camera/rendering:** Wide-angle lenses for claustrophobic interiors (amplifying distortion). Long, slow zooms building tension. Shallow depth of field isolating subjects against threatening backgrounds. Heavy film grain, muted processing. Lens grime and atmospheric haze degrading the image.

## World references
- **Alien (1979):** Industrial spacecraft interiors, dripping chains, motion tracker green, biomechanical Giger corridors, crew as expendable labor
- **Blade Runner (1982):** Acid rain, mega-structures, Tyrell pyramid, replicant eyes glowing, off-world colonies implied but never seen
- **Star Wars — Empire:** Death Star trenches, Star Destroyer interiors, Mustafar lava fields, carbon freezing chambers, Imperial gray uniformity
- **Mad Max — Fury Road:** Bleached desert wastes, rusted war machines, dust storms swallowing the horizon, scarcity as religion
- **The Thing (1982):** Antarctic isolation, paranoia lighting, flesh-horror in fluorescent labs, nowhere to run
- **Terminator (1984):** Skull-littered battlefields under purple skies, chrome endoskeletons in searchlights, Hunter-Killers scanning ruins
- **Brazil (1985):** Kafkaesque bureaucratic interiors, ducts overtaking living spaces, retro-futurist decay
- **District 9:** Alien slums, barbed wire, corrugated metal shanties beside corporate towers, weaponized segregation
