# Deep-focus

Everything sharp from foreground to infinity — Citizen Kane, Ansel Adams' f/64 group, landscape photography.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Stops the lens down to render everything from inches away to infinity in sharp focus simultaneously, allowing complex staging across multiple depth planes.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Total sharpness from near to far
  - Every depth plane equally resolved
  - No hierarchy imposed by focus — all areas equal
  - Allows complex deep staging of action
  - Democratic, observational, comprehensive
- **Reference points:** Gregg Toland (Citizen Kane), Ansel Adams (f/64 group), landscape photography, architectural photography, Jean Renoir
- **Pairs well with:** layered, panoramic, hyper-detailed, vast

## FLUX keywords
deep focus, everything in focus, sharp foreground to background, f/16, deep depth of field, total sharpness, Citizen Kane, landscape sharp, infinite focus
