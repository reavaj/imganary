# Shallow-focus

Razor-thin plane of sharpness isolating the subject — portrait photography, Barry Lyndon's f/0.7, Wong Kar-wai.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Opens the lens aperture to its widest, reducing the depth of field to a razor-thin plane that isolates the subject in sharpness while dissolving everything else into creamy blur.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Subject sharp, background (and often foreground) blurred
  - Creamy, smooth bokeh in out-of-focus areas
  - Strong subject isolation and separation
  - Dreamy, romantic, intimate quality
  - Foreground blur adds depth when present
- **Reference points:** Kubrick (Barry Lyndon, f/0.7), Wong Kar-wai, portrait photography (f/1.2-1.8), macro photography, Terrence Malick
- **Pairs well with:** intimate, glossy, painterly, ethereal

## FLUX keywords
shallow depth of field, shallow focus, bokeh, blurred background, f/1.4, subject isolation, creamy bokeh, out of focus background, portrait lens, selective focus
