# Cross-hatched

Tone built through layered intersecting lines — Dürer's engravings, Moebius' worlds, pen and ink illustration.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Creates tonal value through accumulated layers of parallel lines crossing at angles, where line density equals darkness and line spacing equals light.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Intersecting parallel line sets at varying angles
  - Tone controlled by line density and spacing
  - Directional marks follow form and contour
  - Meticulous, labor-intensive, engraved quality
  - Black ink on white paper aesthetic
- **Reference points:** Albrecht Dürer (engravings), Moebius/Jean Giraud, Gustave Doré, Franklin Booth, pen and ink editorial illustration
- **Pairs well with:** intricate, graphic, sketchy, monochromatic
