# Double-exposed

Two images merged into one frame, ghostly overlay — analog film accidents, album art, Man Ray's experiments.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Superimposes two separate images onto a single frame, creating transparent layering where both images are simultaneously visible, producing dreamlike or poetic associations.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Two distinct images visible at once
  - Transparent, ghostly overlay
  - Dark areas of one image reveal the other
  - Dream logic, visual poetry, association
  - Accidental or intentional depending on context
- **Reference points:** Man Ray (experimental photography), album cover art, Antonio Mora (digital composites), analog film in-camera double exposure
- **Pairs well with:** ethereal, nostalgic, hallucinatory, blurred
