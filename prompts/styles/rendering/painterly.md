# Painterly

Visible brush energy, texture of the hand, art over accuracy — Sargent's portraits, Sorolla's light, Richter's smears.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Makes the act of painting visible in the final image — brushstrokes, color mixing, and gestural energy become part of the subject rather than disappearing behind it.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Visible, energetic brushstrokes
  - Color mixed on the canvas, not blended smooth
  - Gestural, spontaneous application
  - Luminous skin and light from thick, layered paint
  - The hand of the artist is always present
- **Reference points:** John Singer Sargent, Joaquín Sorolla, Gerhard Richter (photo-paintings), Cecily Brown, alla prima technique
- **Pairs well with:** impasto, impressionistic, saturated, warm

painterly, visible brushstrokes, painted, oil painting style, artistic brush, hand-painted, loose painting, alla prima, lush brushwork
