# Volumetric

Visible shafts of light cutting through atmosphere — god rays in fog, dust-filled beams in cathedrals, Ridley Scott's signature haze.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Makes light itself visible by filling the air with particles (fog, dust, smoke, rain) that catch and scatter the beam, creating dramatic three-dimensional shafts.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Visible light beams and god rays
  - Atmospheric medium (fog, smoke, dust, haze)
  - Light has physical presence and volume
  - Depth enhanced by layers of atmosphere
  - Often backlit to maximize beam visibility
- **Reference points:** Ridley Scott (Alien, Blade Runner), David Fincher, cathedral interiors, forest photography, concert lighting
- **Pairs well with:** ominous, ethereal, backlit, deep-focus

## FLUX keywords
god rays, visible light shafts, light beams through fog, atmospheric haze, dust particles catching light, smoke and light, Ridley Scott haze, cathedral light through stained glass
