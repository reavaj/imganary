# Chiaroscuro

Extreme contrast between light and dark, with isolated pools of illumination emerging from deep shadow — the signature of Caravaggio and film noir cinematography.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Plunges most of the frame into darkness while carving subjects out with a single, directional light source, creating sculptural volume and dramatic tension.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Deep, true blacks occupying 60-80% of the frame
  - Faces and bodies half-lit, half-swallowed by shadow
  - Single dominant light source (candle, window, spotlight)
  - Sharp light falloff with minimal fill
  - Skin and surfaces modeled like sculpture
- **Reference points:** Caravaggio, Rembrandt, Gordon Willis (The Godfather), film noir, Georges de La Tour
- **Pairs well with:** candlelit, intimate, painterly, underexposed
