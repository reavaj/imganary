# Harsh

Hard, direct, unforgiving light with razor-edged shadows — the midday sun, the bare bulb, the interrogation lamp.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Produces sharp, clearly defined shadows with abrupt transitions between light and dark, revealing every surface imperfection without mercy.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Razor-sharp shadow edges with no feathering
  - High contrast between lit and unlit areas
  - Unflattering, revealing illumination
  - Squinting subjects, deep eye sockets
  - Emphasizes texture, wrinkles, pores
- **Reference points:** Documentary photography, midday equatorial sun, Weegee flash, Martin Parr, Larry Clark
- **Pairs well with:** gritty, stark, desolate, grainy

## FLUX keywords
harsh lighting, hard light, sharp shadows, direct sunlight, midday sun, strong shadows, unfiltered light, high contrast shadows
