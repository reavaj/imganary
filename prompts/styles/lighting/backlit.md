# Backlit

Subject illuminated from behind, creating glowing edges, rim halos, and silhouetted fronts — the Spielberg sunset, the fashion editorial glow.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Places the primary light source behind the subject, turning them into a luminous outline while their front falls into shadow or requires fill.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Bright halo or rim around the subject's edges
  - Lens flare and light bleed from the source
  - Dark or underexposed front of the subject
  - Glowing hair, fur, or translucent materials
  - Warm golden or cool white depending on source
- **Reference points:** Spielberg (E.T., Empire of the Sun), Emmanuel Lubezki, fashion editorial, sunset photography
- **Pairs well with:** silhouetted, ethereal, warm, rim-lit
