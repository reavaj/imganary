# Underexposed

Crushed shadows and oppressive darkness where detail is swallowed by black — the low-key dread of horror and noir.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Starves the image of light, forcing most tonal information into deep shadow where detail is lost, creating a sense of threat and concealment.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Crushed blacks with minimal shadow detail
  - Isolated highlights emerging from darkness
  - Oppressive, heavy atmosphere
  - Subjects partially hidden or obscured
  - High perceived contrast despite limited tonal range
- **Reference points:** Horror cinematography, film noir, Bradford Young, low-key studio photography, Fincher
- **Pairs well with:** ominous, gritty, harsh, chiaroscuro

## FLUX keywords
underexposed, dark, low key lighting, deep shadows, crushed blacks, moody dark, barely visible, shadow-heavy
