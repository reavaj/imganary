# Gritty

Coarse, rough, unrefined surface quality evoking raw reality — 1970s New Hollywood, street photography, sandpaper.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Introduces an abrasive, unpolished texture that rejects commercial smoothness, grounding the image in physical, confrontational reality.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Coarse, rough, tactile surface quality
  - No polish, no refinement, no flattery
  - Confrontational, street-level energy
  - Dirt, gravel, concrete, asphalt textures
  - Raw, authentic, anti-glamour
- **Reference points:** 1970s New Hollywood (Scorsese, Friedkin), street photography, Larry Clark (Kids), Nan Goldin, concrete brutalism
- **Pairs well with:** grainy, harsh, stark, desaturated

## FLUX keywords
gritty, rough, coarse, raw texture, unpolished, sandpaper texture, rough grain, abrasive, street grit
