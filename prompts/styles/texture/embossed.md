# Embossed

Raised surface pattern pressed from behind or below — letterpress printing, blind deboss, decorative metalwork.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Creates tactile, three-dimensional relief on flat surfaces through pressing, stamping, or molding, adding physical depth that catches raking light.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Raised or recessed pattern on flat material
  - Visible only through shadow and light angle
  - Tactile, inviting touch
  - Craft-oriented, handmade quality
  - Subtle at straight-on, dramatic at raking angles
- **Reference points:** Letterpress printing, blind emboss stationery, medieval seal impressions, decorative tin ceilings, leather tooling
- **Pairs well with:** ornate, intricate, matte, burnished

## FLUX keywords
embossed, raised surface, letterpress, pressed texture, relief surface, tactile, dimensional texture, stamped pattern
