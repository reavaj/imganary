# Metal & Patina

The surfaces of metal — from mirror-polish chrome to deep verdigris patina, forge-scale to brushed aluminum. Age and oxidation as beauty.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Reflective metals need careful lighting — large soft sources for chrome (showing environment reflections), raking light for brushed surfaces (revealing directional grain), overcast for patina (even, detail-rich, no blown highlights). Point sources create dramatic specular hits on polished surfaces.
- **Composition:** Close-up and macro showing surface detail. Contrast between polished and oxidized areas. Metal objects in context (tools on a workbench, copper roof against sky, brass hardware on a wooden door). Reflections in curved metal surfaces distorting the environment.
- **Color palette:** Material-driven. Chrome: neutral mirror. Brass: warm gold. Copper: salmon pink when new, green verdigris when aged. Steel: blue-grey. Iron: orange-red rust. Bronze: dark brown-green. Aluminum: cool silver-white. Gold: warm yellow. The patina tells the age story.
- **Textures & materials:** Mirror-polish chrome (distorted reflections), brushed stainless (directional grain), hammered copper (dimpled, light-catching), forge scale (rough black oxide), rust (orange blooming through paint), verdigris (green copper patina), machine-turned aluminum (spiral pattern), cast iron (rough, granular), gold leaf (thin, crinkled, luminous), solder joints, rivet heads, weld beads.
- **Mood:** Industrial, alchemical, time-weathered. The dignity of worked metal. Craftsmanship visible in every surface. Age as patina, not damage.
- **Reference points:** Industrial photography, clockwork and watchmaking, Richard Serra sculptures, copper-clad architecture, blacksmithing, vintage car restoration
