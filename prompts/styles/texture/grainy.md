# Grainy

Visible film grain or digital noise adding tactile roughness — Tri-X 400 photojournalism, Daido Moriyama's high-contrast streets.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Overlays the image with visible particulate texture from high-ISO film or sensor noise, adding analog warmth, documentary credibility, and raw energy.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Visible random noise/grain pattern across the image
  - Softens fine detail while adding overall texture
  - Analog, handmade, unpolished feeling
  - More prominent in shadows and midtones
  - Associates with urgency, documentary truth
- **Reference points:** Tri-X 400, Daido Moriyama, Larry Clark, Robert Frank, high-ISO photojournalism, 16mm film
- **Pairs well with:** gritty, harsh, nostalgic, desaturated

## FLUX keywords
film grain, grainy, noisy, Tri-X, high ISO, analog film texture, gritty grain, rough texture, photographic noise
