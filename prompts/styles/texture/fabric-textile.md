# Fabric & Textile

The tactile beauty of cloth — drape, weave, texture, transparency. Silk catching light, denim worn soft, wool's warmth made visible.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Raking side light to reveal weave structure and surface texture. Backlight through sheer and translucent fabrics (silk, chiffon, linen). Soft, directional light to show drape and fold shadows. Specular sheen on satin and silk. Matte absorption on wool and cotton.
- **Composition:** Close-up detail shots showing individual thread structure. Full garment shots showing drape and movement (fabric caught mid-flow). Flat lays of folded textiles. Hands touching fabric to convey tactility. Fabric-as-landscape — folds as mountains, creases as valleys.
- **Color palette:** Material-dependent. Raw natural fibers: undyed linen, raw silk, oatmeal wool. Dyed: deep indigo denim, madder red, woad blue. Industrial: crisp white cotton, charcoal suiting. The color comes from the dye, not from digital processing.
- **Textures & materials:** Silk (liquid drape, light-catching sheen), denim (worn soft, faded, white warp threads showing), wool (fuzzy, warm, pills and bobbles), linen (crisp wrinkles, slubby texture), velvet (deep pile, color shift at angles), lace (open structure, shadow patterns), leather (grain, patina, creases), tweed (multi-colored weave), knit (cable, rib, stockinette stitch visible).
- **Mood:** Tactile, sensory, craft-appreciating. You can almost feel the texture through the image. The warmth of wool, the cool of silk, the weight of denim.
- **Reference points:** Fashion detail photography, textile museum archives, Merchant & Mills, Japanese boro and sashiko, Loro Piana, Liberty prints
