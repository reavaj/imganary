# Glass & Transparency

The optical beauty of transparent and translucent materials — refraction, caustics, condensation, frosted glass, crystal, ice.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Backlight and side light reveal transparency best. Light passing through glass creates caustic patterns on nearby surfaces. Rim lighting on glass edges. Specular highlights on curved surfaces. Light refracting and splitting into spectral colors at edges.
- **Composition:** Close-up to mid-range showing the interplay of light through material. Objects seen through or distorted by glass. Layered transparency — glass in front of glass. Condensation droplets as foreground texture. Negative space allowing light to pass through.
- **Textures & materials:** Clear glass (wine glasses, windows, bottles), frosted glass (shower doors, privacy glass), textured glass (cathedral, ribbed, hammered), crystal (cut facets splitting light), ice (clear to cloudy, cracking, melting), water droplets on glass, stained glass, blown glass with internal bubbles, beveled edges catching prismatic light.
- **Mood:** Elegant, precise, optically fascinating. The pleasure of seeing light transformed by material. Clean, luminous, crystalline.
- **Reference points:** Swarovski product photography, cocktail photography, greenhouse interiors, Dale Chihuly blown glass, scientific glassware, ice sculpture

## FLUX keywords
glass, transparency, refraction, caustic light patterns, crystal, frosted glass, light passing through, condensation droplets, prismatic edge, ice, clear liquid, backlit glass, specular highlights, beveled crystal, blown glass bubbles
