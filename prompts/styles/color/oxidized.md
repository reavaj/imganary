# Oxidized

Colors of chemical aging — verdigris green, rust orange, tarnished bronze — the patina of time on metal.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Introduces the distinctive color palette of metal reacting with air and moisture over decades, creating an aged, organic, time-marked quality.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Verdigris (copper green), rust (iron orange-brown), tarnish (silver/bronze darkening)
  - Natural, uncontrolled color transformation
  - Suggests deep time, permanence, history
  - Warm oranges and cool blue-greens coexisting
  - Surface-specific — color comes from material, not light
- **Reference points:** Statue of Liberty patina, rusted industrial landscapes, aged bronze sculptures, Richard Serra weathering steel, Anselm Kiefer
- **Pairs well with:** weathered, decayed, corroded, burnished

## FLUX keywords
oxidized, patina, verdigris, copper green, rust tones, aged metal color, tarnished, weathered copper, bronze patina
