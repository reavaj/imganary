# Complementary

Opposing colors on the color wheel creating maximum chromatic tension — the orange/teal of Hollywood, Vermeer's blue and yellow.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Pairs colors from opposite sides of the color wheel, creating vibrant visual friction that makes both colors appear more intense through mutual contrast.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Two dominant hues that oppose each other (orange/blue, red/green, yellow/purple)
  - Maximum color contrast and energy
  - Neither color recedes — both compete for attention
  - Warm/cool split often divides foreground from background
  - Can feel artificial or cinematic depending on execution
- **Reference points:** Orange/teal Hollywood grading (Michael Bay, Transformers), Vermeer's blue/yellow, Warhol Marilyn prints, sports photography
- **Pairs well with:** saturated, graphic, harsh, neon

## FLUX keywords
complementary colors, orange and teal, color contrast, opposing colors, warm and cool contrast, vibrant color pairing, color tension
