# Watercolor

Transparent, luminous watercolor painting. The medium's natural tendencies — wet-into-wet bleeds, granulation, happy accidents — are features, not bugs.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Light comes from the white of the paper showing through transparent washes. Highlights are unpainted paper. Soft, diffused, airy.
- **Composition:** Loose and organic. Subjects emerge from washes — some edges sharp, most soft and bleeding. Generous white space (unpainted paper) as breathing room.
- **Color palette:** Transparent pigments — cerulean blue, aureolin yellow, permanent rose. Colors mix on the paper, not on the palette. Granulating pigments in shadows (ultramarine, burnt sienna).
- **Textures & materials:** Wet-into-wet blooms and cauliflowers. Dry brush strokes for texture (wood, stone, grass). Visible paper grain (cold-pressed). Salt textures, splatter.
- **Mood:** Spontaneous, ephemeral, poetic. Captures atmosphere over detail.
- **Rendering:** Economy of brushstrokes — suggest rather than define. Darkest darks applied last. No pure black — darkest mixes of complementaries.

## FLUX keywords
watercolor painting, transparent washes, wet-into-wet, paper texture, loose brushwork, plein air, botanical illustration
