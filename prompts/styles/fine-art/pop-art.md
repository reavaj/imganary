# Pop Art

The bold, mass-produced aesthetic of 1960s Pop Art — Warhol's screen prints, Lichtenstein's Ben-Day dots, Hockney's flat California color.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Flat, shadowless. Or dramatically simplified into 2-3 tonal zones. No subtle gradients — light is a design choice, not a simulation.
- **Composition:** Frontal, iconic, poster-like. Single subject fills the frame. Repetition and grid layouts. Bold graphic impact.
- **Color palette:** Loud, saturated, artificial. Magenta, cyan, yellow — process colors pushed to extremes. Complementary color clashes. Black outlines containing flat fills.
- **Textures & materials:** Ben-Day dots (halftone pattern). Screen-print registration marks. Flat unmodulated color. Commercial printing artifacts. Newsprint.
- **Mood:** Ironic, celebratory, subversive. High culture meets commercial culture. Everything is a product.
- **Rendering:** Hard black outlines. Color fills with no blending. Simplified forms — reduce everything to its most recognizable graphic shape. Posterization.
