# Oil Painting

Classical oil painting in the tradition of the Old Masters — Vermeer, Rembrandt, Caravaggio. Rich impasto texture, luminous glazes, centuries of technique.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Chiaroscuro — dramatic contrast between light and shadow. Single warm light source (candle, window). Shadows are deep but never flat black — they glow with reflected color.
- **Composition:** Classical arrangements — golden ratio, triangular groupings. Subjects slightly off-center. Background recedes into atmospheric perspective (sfumato).
- **Color palette:** Earth tones as foundation — raw umber, burnt sienna, yellow ochre. Highlights in warm cream, not pure white. Accents of vermillion, ultramarine, or viridian.
- **Textures & materials:** Visible brushwork and impasto in highlights. Smooth transparent glazes in shadows. Canvas weave visible in thin passages. Craquelure (age cracks) for authenticity.
- **Mood:** Timeless, contemplative, reverent. Quiet drama in ordinary moments.
- **Rendering:** Soft edges where forms turn, sharp edges at points of focus. Skin rendered with translucent layers. Fabric with heavy folds catching light.
