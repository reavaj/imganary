# Steampunk

Victorian-era science fiction — an alternate history where steam power and brass machinery replaced electronics. The aesthetic of Jules Verne, H.G. Wells, and the Great Exhibition of 1851, extrapolated into fantastical technology.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Warm gas lamp glow, amber tungsten, candlelight. Dramatic shadows from industrial machinery. Steam and fog catching light beams. Backlit steam venting from pipes and valves.
- **Composition:** Dense, mechanical, layered. Intricate machinery filling the frame. Subjects surrounded by gears, pipes, pressure gauges. Victorian portrait framing for characters — formal, posed, dignified.
- **Color palette:** Warm metallics dominate — brass, copper, bronze, aged gold. Rich mahogany wood, oxblood leather, dark walnut. Accent with verdigris (oxidized copper green), amber glass, ivory. Deep sepia undertones throughout.
- **Textures & materials:** Riveted brass plates, hand-stitched leather with visible stitching, polished wood grain, blown glass, wrought iron, clockwork gears with visible teeth. Velvet, brocade, and wool fabrics. Patina and age marks — nothing looks factory-new.
- **Mood:** Adventurous, ingenious, romantically industrial. The optimism of the Industrial Revolution applied to impossible machines. Wonder at human craft and invention.
- **Rendering:** Highly detailed — every rivet, every gear tooth, every stitch visible. Mixed media feel — technical illustration meets adventure novel frontispiece. Mechanical components should look functional, not decorative.
- **Costume elements:** Top hats with goggles, corsets with brass fittings, leather aviator caps, waistcoats with watch chains, monocles, brass prosthetics, ray guns with wooden grips, parasols with mechanical features.
