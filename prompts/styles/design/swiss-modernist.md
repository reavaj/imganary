# Swiss Modernist / International Typographic Style

The clean, rational design language of mid-century Swiss graphic design — Müller-Brockmann, Max Bill, Armin Hofmann. Grid systems, sans-serif type, mathematical beauty.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** N/A — this is a graphic design style, not photographic. Flat, diagrammatic. If photography is used, it's high-contrast black and white with no sentimentality.
- **Composition:** Strict grid systems. Asymmetric balance. Strong horizontal and vertical alignment. Text and image unified by the grid. Generous margins.
- **Color palette:** Limited — often two colors plus black and white. Red and black classic. Flat, unmodulated color fields. No gradients, no effects.
- **Textures & materials:** Smooth, clean paper stock. No visible texture — pure form. Occasionally: overprinted transparent color overlaps. Letterpress impression.
- **Mood:** Rational, universal, authoritative. Design as a system, not as expression. Clarity above personality.
- **Rendering:** Geometric abstraction — circles, squares, lines as primary elements. Sans-serif typography as visual architecture. Photography cropped tightly, used as a graphic element.
