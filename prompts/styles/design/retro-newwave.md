# Retro New Wave / Post-Punk

The visual language of early 1980s new wave music — Devo, Talking Heads, Kraftwerk album art. Where Bauhaus geometry meets synthesizer culture.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Flat, even — like cheap studio lighting or early video. Occasional harsh colored gels (red, blue). Fluorescent tube aesthetic.
- **Composition:** Rigid geometric grids, perfect symmetry, mechanical repetition. Subjects posed stiffly like mannequins — deliberately awkward, anti-natural.
- **Color palette:** Primary colors on neutral backgrounds. Red, yellow, blue against grey or white. Or the inverse — grey subjects against saturated color fields. High contrast, no pastels.
- **Textures & materials:** Glossy plastic, smooth vinyl, molded polymer. Industrial materials — corrugated metal, rubber, PVC. Everything looks manufactured and slightly inhuman.
- **Mood:** Ironic, robotic, playfully dystopian. Humans as products, products as art. Detached and deadpan.
- **Rendering:** Clean, graphic, almost diagrammatic. Minimal organic texture. Subjects look like they belong in an instruction manual or corporate training video from an alternate timeline.
