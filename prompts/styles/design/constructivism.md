# Constructivism

Constructivism was a Russian artistic and architectural philosophy that originated in 1915 and flourished in the 1920s. Rejecting purely aesthetic concerns, Constructivists favored art with a social purpose, often celebrating industrial progress and technological advancement. Visually, it is characterized by geometric abstraction, industrial materials, and a dynamic, often fragmented, composition.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Flat, even lighting; strong directional light to emphasize form; stark shadows.
- **Composition:** Dynamic and asymmetric; geometric forms arranged in a non-representational manner; incorporation of industrial elements; emphasis on spatial relationships and tension.
- **Color palette:** Limited to primary colors (red, yellow, blue), black, white, and gray; bold contrasts; occasional use of metallic colors.
- **Textures & materials:** Industrial materials such as metal, glass, and wood; emphasis on the raw, unadorned quality of materials; smooth, polished surfaces juxtaposed with rougher textures.
- **Mood:** Dynamic, energetic, and optimistic; a sense of progress and modernity; a celebration of industrial power; sometimes austere and utilitarian.
- **Rendering:** Geometric abstraction; precise lines and sharp edges; minimal detail; often incorporates collage and photomontage techniques; graphic and bold.
