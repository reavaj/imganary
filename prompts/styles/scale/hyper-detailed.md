# Hyper-detailed

Obsessive level of detail far beyond normal observation — Bosch's hellscapes, Akira's backgrounds, macro photography.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Resolves every surface to an obsessive, almost pathological level of detail, filling the image with more visual information than the eye can process at once.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Every surface fully resolved to microscopic precision
  - Overwhelming quantity of visual information
  - No area left vague or suggested
  - Obsessive, almost compulsive attention
  - Rewards extended viewing with endless discovery
- **Reference points:** Hieronymus Bosch (Garden of Earthly Delights), Akira (background art), Zdzisław Beksiński, macro photography, Albrecht Dürer (The Great Piece of Turf)
- **Pairs well with:** photorealistic, intricate, deep-focus, cluttered

## FLUX keywords
obsessively rendered, every surface resolved, forensic detail, pathological precision, every detail visible, Bosch-level density, microscopic precision, maximum resolution rendering
