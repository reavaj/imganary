# Sparse

Intentionally few elements, emptiness as design choice — zen rock gardens, Ozu interiors, Agnes Martin, desert.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Scatters only a handful of elements across the frame with deliberate, considered placement, making the space between objects as important as the objects themselves.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Very few objects, widely spaced
  - Each element placed with extreme intention
  - Space between things is the primary subject
  - Quiet, contemplative, still
  - Absence communicates as strongly as presence
- **Reference points:** Zen rock gardens (Ryōan-ji), Yasujirō Ozu (interior compositions), Agnes Martin (grids), desert landscapes, Giorgio Morandi (still life)
- **Pairs well with:** minimal, serene, austere, muted

## FLUX keywords
sparse, few elements, empty space, minimal objects, scattered, isolated elements, bare, open, uncluttered, lonely objects
