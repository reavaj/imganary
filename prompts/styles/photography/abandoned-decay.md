# Abandoned / Decay

The beauty of deterioration — peeling paint, crumbling plaster, rust blooms, nature reclaiming architecture. Ruins as subject, not backdrop.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Shafts of light through broken roofs and shattered windows. Overcast diffuse light revealing texture without harsh shadows. Green-filtered light through overgrown windows. Dust motes visible in light beams.
- **Composition:** Wide establishing shots showing scale of abandonment. Detail shots of specific decay — a single doorknob consumed by rust, paint curling off a wall. Symmetry broken by collapse. Nature and architecture in tension — tree roots splitting tile floors, vines pulling down ceilings.
- **Color palette:** Desaturated earth tones — rust orange, mold green, concrete grey, plaster white. Occasional punch from surviving paint or graffiti. Blue-green patina on copper. Yellow-brown water stains. The palette of oxidation and organic reclamation.
- **Textures & materials:** Peeling paint in thick layers revealing decades of color underneath. Crumbling plaster exposing brick and lath. Rust blooming through metal. Broken glass. Moss and lichen on stone. Rotting wood grain. Standing water reflecting ceilings. Fallen ceiling tiles. Dust on every surface.
- **Mood:** Melancholic beauty, time made visible. The sublime tension between what was built and what nature is taking back. Quiet, reverent, archaeological.
- **Reference points:** Pripyat/Chernobyl photography, Detroit ruin porn, Hashima Island, Yves Marchand & Romain Meffre, Andrew Moore (Detroit Disassembled), Chris Payne
