# Portrait Photography

Environmental and studio portraiture — the craft of revealing character through light, background, and moment. Not fashion, not glamour — honest human photography.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Rembrandt lighting (triangle of light on shadow-side cheek), split lighting, or broad window light. Key light with subtle fill. Catchlights in eyes. Light that sculpts the face dimensionally, not flattens it.
- **Composition:** Head-and-shoulders or three-quarter body. Subject's environment tells their story (workshop, kitchen, street, office). Eye-level or slightly above. Shallow depth of field separating subject from background. Rule of thirds placement.
- **Color palette:** Natural, warm-neutral skin tones. Background colors complement but don't compete. Muted environments that push the face forward. Film-stock tonality — Portra warmth or Tri-X grain.
- **Textures & materials:** Skin texture visible — pores, lines, stubble, freckles. Fabric texture in clothing. Environmental detail (tools, books, instruments) contextualizes the subject. Hands as expressive as the face.
- **Mood:** Intimate, revealing, respectful. The subject seen clearly, not idealized. A real person in their real context.
- **Reference points:** Arnold Newman (environmental portraits), Annie Leibovitz, Steve McCurry (Afghan Girl), Platon, Dorothea Lange, Yousuf Karsh
