# Golden Hour

The last hour before sunset — warm amber light, long shadows, everything dipped in honey. The most photogenic natural lighting condition.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Low-angle sun at 15-30 degrees above horizon. Warm amber-orange at ~3000K color temperature. Long, dramatic shadows stretching across surfaces. Rim light and backlighting making hair and edges glow. Warm bounce fill from sunlit surfaces.
- **Composition:** Backlit subjects with sun flare at frame edge. Long shadow lines as compositional elements. Silhouettes against golden sky. Sun peeking through trees, buildings, or between fingers.
- **Color palette:** Amber, gold, warm orange, honey. Shadows go blue-purple (complementary to the golden highlights). Skin tones deeply warm, almost bronze. Sky gradients from deep blue zenith to orange horizon.
- **Textures & materials:** Warm light reveals surface texture — bark, stone, fabric, skin all gain dimensionality from raking side light. Lens flare and sun streaks. Dust motes or pollen caught in light beams.
- **Mood:** Nostalgic, romantic, fleeting. The feeling of a perfect moment about to end.
- **Reference points:** Terrence Malick (Days of Heaven, Badlands), Emmanuel Lubezki, Instagram golden hour, landscape photography, Kodak Gold film
