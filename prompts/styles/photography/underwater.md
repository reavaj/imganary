# Underwater

The alien world beneath the surface — caustic light patterns, suspended particles, blue-green depth, weightless movement.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Caustic light patterns rippling across surfaces from above. Sun beams penetrating from the surface in visible shafts. Light attenuates with depth — bright near surface, deep blue-black below. Bioluminescence in deep water. Backlight through translucent subjects (jellyfish, kelp, fins).
- **Composition:** Looking up toward the bright surface (Snell's window). Subjects suspended in three-dimensional space — no ground plane. Bubbles as compositional elements rising through frame. Coral reef complexity filling the frame. Open water negative space creating isolation.
- **Color palette:** Blue-green dominant. Warm colors (red, orange) disappear with depth — only blue and green remain below 10m. Near surface: full color with cyan bias. Deep water: monochromatic blue-black. Artificial light (dive torch, flash) restores warm colors in a small pool against the blue.
- **Textures & materials:** Coral structure — brain coral, branching coral, sea fans. Scales and skin texture on marine life. Bubble streams. Sand particles suspended in water. Kelp forest canopy. Jellyfish translucency. Anemone tentacles. Water itself as a visible medium — particulate, plankton, visibility gradient.
- **Mood:** Alien, serene, vast. The feeling of floating in another world. Time slows down underwater.
- **Reference points:** David Doubilet (National Geographic), Brian Skerry, Planet Earth II ocean episodes, Jacques Cousteau, Zena Holloway (fashion underwater)
