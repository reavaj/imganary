# Food Photography

Editorial food photography — the aesthetic of Bon Appétit, Ottolenghi cookbooks, and restaurant menus where every ingredient is hero-lit.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Soft, directional side light or backlight to reveal texture. Warm, natural — window light or bounced diffuser. Steam and condensation lit from behind to glow. Specular highlights on wet surfaces and glazes.
- **Composition:** Overhead flat lay or 45-degree hero angle. Shallow depth of field isolating the hero dish. Negative space balanced by scattered ingredients or utensils. Hands mid-action (pouring, breaking bread, drizzling sauce) for editorial energy.
- **Color palette:** Natural, warm, appetizing. Greens vibrant but not neon. Warm wood, linen, ceramic neutrals. Pops of color from fresh herbs, citrus, chili. No over-processed HDR glow.
- **Textures & materials:** Glistening sauce, caramelized crust, bubbling cheese, fresh herb leaves, cracked pepper, flaky pastry, steam rising, condensation on glass. Rustic table surfaces — reclaimed wood, linen napkin, handmade ceramic plate, cast iron skillet.
- **Mood:** Appetite-inducing, honest, tactile. The kind of food photo that makes you stop scrolling.
- **Reference points:** Bon Appétit Test Kitchen, Ottolenghi, David Chang, Christina Tosi, J. Kenji López-Alt
