# Rain / Wet Surfaces

Rain-soaked scenes where every surface becomes a mirror — wet pavement reflections, rain-streaked glass, glistening skin, puddle doubles.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Reflections multiply every light source. Streetlights become elongated streaks on wet asphalt. Neon and signage reflected in puddles. Diffused overcast sky or nighttime with point sources. Specular highlights everywhere.
- **Composition:** Low angles to catch puddle reflections. Through-the-window shots with rain droplets on glass. Reflections creating vertical symmetry on wet streets. Umbrellas as compositional elements.
- **Color palette:** Desaturated daytime (overcast grey-blue) or warm nighttime (sodium orange, neon reflected in water). High contrast between dark wet surfaces and bright reflected lights. Everything slightly muted by atmosphere.
- **Textures & materials:** Wet concrete, rain-slicked asphalt, droplets on glass, puddles, wet hair plastered to skin, soaked fabric clinging to body, water beading on leather or metal, rivulets running down surfaces.
- **Mood:** Contemplative, atmospheric, cinematic. The romance and melancholy of being caught in rain.
- **Reference points:** Blade Runner (rain-soaked LA), Saul Leiter (rain through windows), Gregory Halpern, Tokyo street photography, Wong Kar-wai

## FLUX keywords
rain, wet pavement, puddle reflections, rain-slicked street, water droplets on glass, glistening wet surfaces, rain-soaked, overcast, streetlights reflected in wet asphalt, dripping water, soaked fabric, atmospheric rain, low angle reflection
