# Macro / Close-Up

Extreme close-up photography revealing worlds invisible to the naked eye — surface textures, tiny subjects, material detail at obsessive magnification.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Ring light or twin flash for even illumination of tiny subjects. Raking side light to reveal surface texture. Backlight through translucent subjects (leaves, wings, petals). Focus stacking for maximum depth.
- **Composition:** Subject fills the frame at 1:1 magnification or greater. Razor-thin plane of focus with everything else dissolved into bokeh. Geometric patterns in organic structures (compound eyes, leaf veins, crystal lattice). Negative space from out-of-focus backgrounds.
- **Color palette:** Whatever the subject dictates, but rendered with extreme fidelity. Iridescence in insect wings, subtle color shifts in metal surfaces, translucency in botanical subjects. No color grading — let the material speak.
- **Textures & materials:** Water droplets on petals, insect compound eyes, fabric weave at thread level, watch mechanism gears, rust and patina close-up, frost crystals, food surfaces (bread crust, citrus pores, chocolate snap). Surface detail beyond what the eye normally resolves.
- **Mood:** Revelatory, alien, scientific wonder. Familiar things made strange through proximity.
- **Reference points:** Levon Biss (microsculpture), Karl Blossfeldt (botanical close-ups), National Geographic macro, watchmaking photography, electron microscope aesthetic

## FLUX keywords
