# 1970s Period Photography

The look and feel of the 1970s captured through its own photographic technology — Kodachrome warmth, wide collars, wood paneling, harvest gold everything.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Warm tungsten interiors with orange cast. Harsh midday sun with deep shadows outdoors. No LED, no modern color temperature — everything leans warm. Fluorescent office lighting with green tint. Available light, rarely flash.
- **Composition:** Snapshot aesthetic — slightly off-center, casual framing. Eye-level or slightly below. Interiors show the full room (conversation pit, wood paneling, shag carpet). Cars photographed from low angles. People posed but relaxed, cigarette in hand.
- **Color palette:** Kodachrome: saturated but warm, burnt orange, avocado green, harvest gold, brown, rust. Faded with age — slightly yellowed, warm color shift. Earth tones dominate fashion and interiors. Denim blue as the one cool accent. Wood-tone everything.
- **Textures & materials:** Shag carpet, wood paneling, vinyl upholstery, corduroy, denim, macramé, ceramic ashtrays, rotary phones, analog TV screens, tube amplifiers, vinyl records, chrome bumpers on land-yacht cars. Cigarette smoke in the air. Brown glass and amber bottles.
- **Mood:** Nostalgic, warm, slightly grimy. The decade before everything went digital — analog textures, manual everything, tactile surfaces. Pre-renovation, pre-gentrification.
- **Reference points:** William Eggleston, Stephen Shore (Uncommon Places), 1970s National Geographic, Starsky & Hutch, The French Connection, Kodachrome slide collections
