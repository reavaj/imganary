# Film Noir

The chiaroscuro world of 1940s-50s crime cinema. Dramatic shadow play, moral ambiguity rendered in light and dark.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Single hard key light casting long shadows. Venetian blind patterns on walls. Practical lights — desk lamps, neon signs — as motivated sources. Deep blacks.
- **Composition:** Dutch angles, low camera positions looking up at imposing figures. Deep staging with foreground silhouettes framing background action. Strong diagonal lines.
- **Color palette:** Black and white with full tonal range. If color: desaturated with teal shadows and amber highlights (neo-noir). Smoke and fog catching light beams.
- **Textures & materials:** Wet cobblestones, trench coat wool, cigarette smoke, rain-streaked glass, fedora felt. Everything slightly damp.
- **Mood:** Paranoid, seductive, fatalistic. Danger in every shadow.
- **Camera:** Deep focus cinematography (everything sharp). Wide-angle distortion on close-ups. Heavy film grain.

## FLUX keywords
film noir, chiaroscuro, dramatic shadows, black and white, 1940s, venetian blind lighting, detective, moody
