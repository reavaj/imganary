# Interior Design

Architectural interior photography — the craft of capturing designed spaces. Mid-century modern, Scandinavian, wabi-sabi, maximalist eclectic.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Natural window light flooding in, mixed with warm artificial fixtures. Time of day matters — morning light vs. evening lamp glow. Light pools and shadow patterns from windows on floors and walls. No harsh flash — ambient only.
- **Composition:** One-point perspective down hallways and into rooms. Wide angle but corrected verticals (no keystone distortion). Styled but lived-in — a draped throw, an open book, a half-drunk coffee. Layered depth: foreground object, midground furniture, background architectural detail.
- **Color palette:** Cohesive and curated — warm wood tones with muted textiles, or monochromatic with texture variation. Material palette tells the design story: concrete + brass + linen, or oak + ceramic + wool. Whites that aren't pure white (warm ivory, cool grey-white).
- **Textures & materials:** Tactile surfaces dominate — woven rugs, turned wood legs, patinated brass hardware, raw linen, fired ceramic, veined marble, hand-plastered walls, poured concrete, oiled leather. FLUX excels at rendering these materials.
- **Mood:** Aspirational but inhabitable. You want to sit in that chair, drink from that mug, read by that window. Warm, considered, designed.
- **Reference points:** Architectural Digest, Kinfolk, Cereal Magazine, Tadao Ando interiors, Axel Vervoordt, vintage Scandinavian design
