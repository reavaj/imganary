# Organic

Flowing, natural, curved forms derived from biological shapes — Art Nouveau, Gaudi's architecture, botanical illustration.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Replaces rigid geometry with curves, tendrils, and growth patterns found in nature, creating a living, breathing, biomorphic visual language.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Curves, spirals, tendrils, growth patterns
  - Asymmetrical but balanced like a plant
  - No straight lines or hard corners
  - Living, breathing, growing quality
  - Nature as design template
- **Reference points:** Art Nouveau (Gaudi, Mucha, Hector Guimard), botanical illustration, Zaha Hadid, Ernst Haeckel, biomorphic sculpture (Henry Moore)
- **Pairs well with:** sinuous, intricate, ethereal, impressionistic

## FLUX keywords
organic shapes, flowing curves, natural forms, biomorphic, Art Nouveau, botanical, curvilinear, living shapes, growth forms, nature-inspired
