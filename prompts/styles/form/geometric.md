# Geometric

Hard-edged mathematical shapes as primary visual element — Mondrian, Bauhaus, Islamic tessellation, Sol LeWitt.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Organizes the image around precise mathematical forms — circles, squares, triangles, grids — creating order, rationality, and visual clarity through geometric purity.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Circles, squares, triangles as building blocks
  - Precise, measured, mathematical edges
  - Grid-based underlying structure
  - Rational, ordered, systematic
  - Pattern and repetition through geometric logic
- **Reference points:** Piet Mondrian, Bauhaus (Kandinsky, Klee), Islamic geometric tilework, Sol LeWitt, Bridget Riley (Op Art)
- **Pairs well with:** minimal, symmetrical, graphic, austere

## FLUX keywords
geometric, geometric shapes, mathematical, Mondrian, grid, circles and squares, precise geometry, hard-edged, tessellated, pattern geometry
