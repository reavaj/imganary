# Metabolist / Japanese Futurism

The capsule-based, organic-meets-machine architecture of 1960s-70s Japan — Nakagin Capsule Tower, Osaka Expo '70. Cities as living organisms that grow, transform, and replace parts.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Overcast Tokyo light or harsh fluorescent. Night scenes with warm capsule windows glowing against concrete megastructures. Mixed artificial light sources — tungsten, neon, fluorescent.
- **Composition:** Dense, stacked, modular. Capsules and pods clustered like biological cells. Infrastructure visible — pipes, ducts, walkways connecting units. Vertical sprawl.
- **Color palette:** Concrete grey and off-white as base. Accent colors from capsule interiors — warm orange, avocado green, mustard yellow (1970s palette). Oxidized metal patina. Faded signage.
- **Textures & materials:** Prefabricated steel capsules, fiberglass pods, exposed utility conduits. Round porthole windows. Rubber gaskets, riveted panels. Weathered but optimistic — retro-futurism aging in real time.
- **Mood:** Utopian nostalgia for a future that almost happened. Compact, ingenious, slightly melancholic. The optimism of the space age applied to urban living.
- **Architectural elements:** Plug-in capsules, megastructure cores, pedestrian skyways, modular units, circular windows, elevated highways, geodesic elements.
