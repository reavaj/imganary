# Brutalist Architecture

The raw concrete monumentalism of post-war Eastern European and Soviet civic buildings — unfinished béton brut, massive geometric forms, social housing blocks, and socialist realist public spaces.

## Visual DNA
- **Lighting:** Overcast, flat grey skies that merge with the concrete. Or harsh low-angle sun raking across textured surfaces, creating deep shadows in recesses and reveal lines. Golden hour on raw concrete turns it warm amber.
- **Composition:** Low angle looking upward to emphasize mass and scale. Symmetrical facades. Repetitive modular grids — identical windows, balconies, stairwells receding into perspective. Human figures tiny against monumental forms.
- **Color palette:** Concrete grey as the dominant tone — warm grey, cool grey, stained grey. Muted earth tones. Oxidized copper green, rusted steel orange. Pops of faded socialist-era signage (red, yellow). Overcast skies in blue-grey.
- **Textures & materials:** Board-formed concrete showing wood grain impressions. Exposed aggregate. Poured concrete with water stains, moss, and weathering. Raw steel, wire glass, precast panels with visible seams. Terrazzo floors, ceramic tile mosaics.
- **Mood:** Monumental, austere, melancholic. Equal parts awe and alienation. The weight of ideology made physical.
- **Architectural elements:** Cantilevered volumes, pilotis (columns lifting mass off ground), deep window reveals, covered walkways, ceremonial plazas, elevated pedestrian decks, circular ramps.
