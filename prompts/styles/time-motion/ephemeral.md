# Ephemeral

Fleeting, dissolving, about to vanish — smoke photography, James Turrell, soap bubbles, cherry blossoms.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Captures subjects in the act of disappearing — fading, dissolving, evaporating — creating a poignant sense of transience and the fragility of the present moment.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Subjects fading, dissolving, or evaporating
  - Edges soft, disappearing into background
  - Transparency and impermanence visible
  - Fragile beauty that won't last
  - Caught just before vanishing
- **Reference points:** Smoke photography, James Turrell (light installations), soap bubble photography, cherry blossom (mono no aware), Andy Goldsworthy (ephemeral land art)
- **Pairs well with:** ethereal, translucent, soft-focus, blurred

## FLUX keywords
ephemeral, fleeting, vanishing, dissolving, fading, transient, momentary, disappearing, fragile, about to vanish, smoke-like
