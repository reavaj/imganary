# Asymmetrical

Deliberate imbalance creating dynamic visual tension — Degas' cropped dancers, Japanese ikebana, editorial layout.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Weights the visual mass to one side of the frame, using empty space as counterbalance to create energy, movement, and a sense of caught-in-the-moment spontaneity.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Subject placed off-center, often at edge
  - Empty space as active compositional element
  - Dynamic tension between mass and void
  - Implied movement or narrative direction
  - More energetic and spontaneous than centered
- **Reference points:** Degas (cropped ballet paintings), Japanese ikebana, editorial magazine layouts, Henri Cartier-Bresson, Saul Leiter
- **Pairs well with:** cropped, fragmented, organic, intimate

## FLUX keywords
asymmetrical, off-center, unbalanced composition, dynamic framing, rule of thirds, weighted to one side, editorial composition, tension
