# Cropped

Tight framing that cuts into the subject, showing only a fragment — Degas' ballet edges, fashion close-ups, extreme detail.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Cuts aggressively into the subject at the frame edges, showing only a portion and implying the rest exists beyond the visible boundary, creating tension and intimacy.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Subject extends beyond frame edges, visibly cut off
  - Tension between what's shown and what's implied
  - Intimacy through forced proximity
  - Subjects feel larger than the frame can contain
  - Edge-of-frame energy and dynamism
- **Reference points:** Degas (ballet paintings, cropped figures), fashion photography close-ups, extreme macro, Robert Frank (cropped spontaneity)
- **Pairs well with:** intimate, asymmetrical, shallow-focus, angular

## FLUX keywords
cropped, tight framing, close crop, cut off, partial view, edge of frame, tight composition, extreme close-up, fragmentary
