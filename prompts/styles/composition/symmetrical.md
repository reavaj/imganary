# Symmetrical

Obsessive bilateral balance, dead-center subjects — Kubrick's hallways, Wes Anderson's frames, Byzantine icons.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Divides the frame into mirror-image halves along a central axis, creating a sense of perfection, control, and occasionally unsettling orderliness.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Perfect bilateral balance left-to-right
  - Subject centered on the vertical axis
  - Architectural, geometric precision
  - Can feel calming (temples) or threatening (institutions)
  - Demands perfectly level camera
- **Reference points:** Kubrick (The Shining, 2001), Wes Anderson, Taj Mahal, Byzantine icons, Peter Greenaway
- **Pairs well with:** austere, monumental, stark, receding

## FLUX keywords
symmetrical, perfect symmetry, centered composition, mirror image, bilateral, balanced, centered framing, Kubrick symmetry, architectural symmetry
