# Fragmented

Image broken into pieces, multiple viewpoints simultaneously — Cubist painting, David Carson layouts, Hockney joiners.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Shatters the single-viewpoint image into multiple overlapping or adjacent fragments, showing the subject from several angles or moments at once.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Multiple viewpoints visible simultaneously
  - Broken, shattered, or collaged composition
  - Non-linear visual reading path
  - Deconstructed, reassembled logic
  - Each fragment is a valid image; together they redefine the whole
- **Reference points:** Picasso and Braque (Cubism), David Carson (Ray Gun magazine), David Hockney (photo joiners), Hannah Höch (Dada photomontage)
- **Pairs well with:** angular, graphic, expressionistic, double-exposed

## FLUX keywords
fragmented, cubist, broken apart, shattered, multiple angles, collage, fractured, deconstructed, split view, pieced together
