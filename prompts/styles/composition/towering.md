# Towering

Extreme low-angle vertical emphasis, subjects soaring upward — Gothic cathedrals, skyscraper photography, Metropolis.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Tilts the camera dramatically upward, stretching vertical lines and making subjects loom overhead, creating a visceral sense of height, power, and viewer insignificance.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - Extreme low-angle looking up
  - Vertical lines dominating, converging upward
  - Subject appears to soar, loom, or tower
  - Viewer feels small, overwhelmed, subordinate
  - Vertigo-inducing height and scale
- **Reference points:** Gothic cathedral photography, Metropolis (Fritz Lang), skyscraper photography (Berenice Abbott), redwood forest photography, brutalist towers
- **Pairs well with:** monumental, angular, monolithic, vast

## FLUX keywords
towering, low angle, looking up, soaring, vertical, tall, imposing height, upward perspective, looming above, dramatic low angle
