# Minimal

Maximum negative space, fewest possible elements — Swiss design, Sugimoto's horizons, Agnes Martin's grids.

## Visual DNA
- **What it does:** Reduces the image to its absolute essential elements, using vast negative space to give the remaining subject maximum visual weight and breathing room.
- **Key characteristics:**
  - 80%+ of the frame is empty or uniform
  - One or two focal elements, no more
  - Negative space is the primary compositional tool
  - Clean, uncluttered, reductive
  - Every remaining element carries enormous weight
- **Reference points:** Swiss International Style, Hiroshi Sugimoto (Seascapes), Agnes Martin, Dieter Rams, Apple product photography
- **Pairs well with:** austere, serene, matte, stark

## FLUX keywords
minimal, minimalist, negative space, empty, simple composition, sparse, clean, breathing room, one element, vast empty space
